# Authorship and Credit

Academic credit is real currency, and this project spends it honestly. This
policy exists so that anyone considering a contribution knows, **before they
start**, what it earns.

## The rule

Credit follows the [CRediT taxonomy](https://credit.niso.org/). For any paper,
preprint, or versioned research report produced by this project:

**Co-authorship** is offered to anyone whose contribution meets *both*:

1. A substantive intellectual contribution to that work — any of:
   conceptualization, methodology, software that generates results in the
   paper, formal analysis, investigation, validation/verification that
   changed the work (including a critique that materially altered a claim or
   model), data curation, or writing.
2. Participation in revising the work and approval of the final version
   (every author must be able to stand behind the whole).

**Acknowledgment** covers: reproduction reports confirming existing results,
review comments that improved clarity, infrastructure and tooling not specific
to the paper's results, funding, and discussions.

**Citation** covers: prior published work the paper builds on, including
contributions already credited in an earlier versioned report.

## Mechanics

- Each paper effort opens a public **contributor call** issue when work
  begins, naming the scope and the planned author list, updated as work
  proceeds. No one discovers at submission time that they are — or are not —
  an author.
- Author order: first author is the lead of that work; others follow
  contribution size, ties broken alphabetically; disagreements are settled by
  the process in [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) *before* submission.
- Contributors are asked to provide an **ORCID** when joining a paper effort.
- Every release and paper carries a contributors section naming everyone at
  acknowledgment level or above; `MEMBERS.md` and the git history are the
  permanent record.
- **Negative results carry authorship too.** If your investigation kills a
  claim and that finding is published (it will be — see the
  [Charter](CHARTER.md), §4.2), you are an author of that publication under
  the same criteria.

## AI assistance

The project's manuscripts disclose their AI assistance on page one, and any
future submission will carry whatever AI-disclosure statement the target
venue requires. Contributors' substantial AI assistance is disclosed the same
way (see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)). Disclosure is about honesty,
not stigma: the standard here is that a human author can defend every claim.
