## 3. Is the −0.95 dB a constant offset on gain(F)? — CONFIRMED (in-model)

*(Naming: "bulk-viscous" here means the **volume/collision** channel — the shear/**kinematic** viscosity `ν = v_F²·τ_ee/4` that 07-13 §A.3 derives as first-sound damping — as distinct from the boundary/contact channel, following the promoted 07-18 §4 usage. It is **not** the thermodynamic bulk viscosity ζ, which 07-13 sets ≈ 0 for the scale-invariant Dirac cone. The −0.95 dB is the kinematic-viscous volume term.)*

The dissent treated it as a constant shift; the prompt asks whether the fan-out's softened operating point moves it. **It does not — and the physics says it is, if anything, *more* reliable at high F.** `γ_visc` (the viscous damping behind the −0.95 dB) is `ν·q²·τ` with `ν = v_F²·τ_ee/4` ([`kinetic.viscous_fraction`](https://github.com/ryoji-info/FableComputer/blob/main/fable-model-chain/kinetic.py)) — a **linear-response** rate set by `τ_ee(T)`, `q` (geometry), `τ(T)`, and the DC drift M through the even-in-M factor. It is **amplitude-independent**. The fan-out changes only the *AC signal amplitude* each branch receives (`A_op·F^(−½)`); it does not change `γ_visc`, the DC drift M (tracked to 0.7·M_th, F-invariant), or T. The one coupling the prompt flags — the in-situ M_th tracking — does not break it: the −0.95 dB is *already* the tracked-bias effect (07-13's tracked −0.284 dB/% vs frozen −0.901 dB/% already carries the re-tracking), and the upstream cell's threshold is set by its own cavity, not its fan-out load, so the shift is F-invariant. **The −0.95 dB is a rigid vertical offset on the entire gain(F) curve** (in-model). Nuance worth stating: the F-split moves each branch *below* the 1-dB knee (softer, more linear drive), so the linear-response assumption underpinning the offset is *better* satisfied at F = 2–4 than at F = 1 — the composition is not a high-F extrapolation, it is a high-F *strength*.

## 4. The depth/noise dimension: a hard disorder floor binds fan-out at F = 2 (demonstrated)

The 07-20 note argued F = 2 only softly, via the fractional noise margin. The released disorder model makes it **hard and code-grounded** — with one in-model composition step, stated up front. [`disorder.py`](https://github.com/ryoji-info/FableComputer/blob/main/fable-model-chain/disorder.py) states the regeneration criterion outright (demonstrated): a cell regenerates only if its sharpness clears the floor `K_MIN = 5.0` (with design `K0 = 8.0`), and the module's own comment notes this `k` "sets the static noise margin **and** the disorder tolerance". The composition (in-model): the fan-out softens the *input* sharpness to `k_eff = 8·F^(−½)` (the 07-20 model), and I take that softened `k_eff` as the sharpness the floor acts on — i.e. `disorder.py`'s design-sharpness slot `K0` is fed the fan-out-softened value. This equates the fan-out input-eye sharpness with the cell transfer sharpness (both are `cell.py`'s single `k = β·A_swing/dn`); it is code-consistent but not independently derived (Limitation 3). Under it, the downstream cell must clear the floor with its softened sharpness:

| F | `k_eff = 8·F^(−½)` | clears `k_min=5`? | σ_crit (n=1e12) | literature-puddle yield |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 8.00 | yes (+3.0) | 1.56×10¹¹ cm⁻² | 100 % |
| 2 | **5.66** | yes (+0.66) | 9.35×10¹⁰ cm⁻² | 100 % |
| 3 | **4.62** | **NO — fails at zero puddle** | imaginary | **0 %** |
| 4 | 4.00 | NO | — | 0 % |

**⟹ Fan-out ceiling from the disorder floor: `F ≤ (8/5)² = 2.56` ⟹ F_max = 2** (`k_min` and the arithmetic demonstrated; the binding is in-model, given the `k_eff → K0` substitution above). This is *independent* of the gain-margin ledger and *harder*: below it, the downstream cell does not regenerate on *any* substrate, clean or dirty — F = 3 fails even at zero charge puddle (MC yield 0 %, §6). It converges with the near-specular gain-reserved F = 2, which is the point: **two independent constraints — a thin gain reserve and a sharpness floor — both cap the honest fan-out at 2, so even a full rejection of the `k_eff → K0` substitution leaves the F = 2 ceiling standing on the +2.0 dB gain reserve alone.**

**Depth D, honest determination.** For a *restoring* cascade with per-cell margin > 0 and `k_eff ≥ k_min`, each stage re-establishes full logic swing, so depth is **not gain-limited** (a restoring gate does not accumulate gain error the way a passive attenuator does — mean depth is unbounded in the gain sense once margin > 0). What binds is **noise**: the per-stage probability that a jitter/thermal/disorder excursion exceeds the static noise margin. The gain-recovery ISI is negligible at the operating symbol rate (`cell.isi_spread_dB` = **0.0001 dB @ 0.25 THz, 0.0218 dB @ 1 THz**, demonstrated), so ISI does not set depth at F = 1–2. **The binding constraint is the noise/disorder margin, and the F-split erodes it in lockstep with the gain reserve** (F=1: k_eff=8.0, NM=0.256, σ_crit=1.56e11; F=2: k_eff=5.66, NM=0.221 = 86 %, σ_crit halved to 9.35e10). **Joint (F, D) envelope (in-model):** *F=1* — full margin, deepest cascade (D limited only by a per-stage BER the static chain does not quantify → **D open but large**); *F=2* — near-specular only, thin gain reserve (+2.0 dB) and `k_eff` just above the disorder floor, literature-puddle yield still 100 % but σ_crit halved → viable but the least-margin corner; *F≥3* — downstream cell fails the disorder floor → **D = 0, cascade broken, regardless of gain.** The exact D number is **open**: it needs a per-stage BER/jitter model (a loaded-cascade transient with disorder), the WP1 check this predicts.

## 5. One logic-family comparison: RSFQ's fan-out-1 discipline

RSFQ restores logic by the single-flux-quantum: each junction either transmits a quantized Φ₀ pulse or not, a hard digital snap with no linear-compression analog. Its regeneration is robust, but its **fan-out is exactly 1** — a single SFQ pulse is a conserved quantum and cannot drive two loads; RSFQ *must* spend an active splitter/JTL (Josephson transmission line) tree to fan out, at real area, bias, and static-power cost (Likharev & Semenov 1991; the fan-out-1 discipline is textbook). The Fable fabric's *analog regenerative* gain buys the opposite trade: **one cell genuinely drives F = 2 with no active splitter** (the −10·log₁₀F power split is passive), which is architecturally cheaper than RSFQ's splitter tree — *but* the price is precisely the margin this reply quantifies: the fan-out is soft (it erodes `k_eff` toward the disorder floor and thins the gain reserve), where RSFQ's fan-out-1 is hard but splitter-recoverable. **The honest architecture statement:** the fabric buys passive fan-out-2 that RSFQ can only buy actively, but only at a near-specular contact and within a razor-thin composed reserve; neither family escapes paying for fan-out, and this fabric pays in contact quality and disorder headroom rather than in splitter hardware. (Fan-out is a feasibility gate, never a performance claim — rule 6; and all of this rides on the unproven gain cell.)

```python
# Reproduces the 07-20 ledger AND folds in the promoted -0.95 dB (band [-1.5,-0.3])

import math, os, sys, json
CHAIN = r"...\fable-model-chain"                        # adjust to your checkout
sys.path.insert(0, CHAIN)
import constants as C, regen as R, cell as CELL, kinetic as K, disorder as D
RES = json.load(open(os.path.join(CHAIN, "results.json"), encoding="utf-8"))
G   = R.cw_net_gain_dB(0.7);  PGL = RES["per_gate_loss_353K_dB"];  A_op = 0.0116
ASAT_CW, ASAT_PULSE = 0.015002, 0.022575
comp = lambda s, asat: 20*math.log10(1.0/math.sqrt(1+(s/asat)**2))
gain = lambda F, asat: G + comp(A_op*F**-0.5, asat)
nm   = lambda k: 0.5*(1-2.0/k)*(1-10**(-0.5));  keff = lambda F: 8.0*F**-0.5
# (A) anchors
print("G=%.6f(json %.6f) PGL=%.6f NM8=%.6f(json %.6f) cascade0=%.4f(json %.4f)"
      % (G, RES["cw_regen_gain_dB_at_0p7"], PGL, CELL.static_noise_margin(),
         RES["noise_margin_frac"], gain(1,0.02), RES["cascade_per_cell_dB"]["0"]))
print("knee F=1: CW=%.2f(7.63) pulse=%.2f(8.64)" % (gain(1,ASAT_CW), gain(1,ASAT_PULSE)))
# (B) composed ledger
BULK=-0.95; CORNERS=[("near-spec",0.0),("07-18 pess",-1.0),("Kn2 p=2",-1.67),("Kn p=1",-5.73)]
def ledger(asat,bulk,bnd):
    rows=[(F, round(gain(F,asat)+bulk+bnd-10*math.log10(F)-PGL,2)) for F in (1,2,3,4,5)]
    raw=max((F for F,m in rows if m>=0),default=0); res=max((F for F,m in rows if m>=2.0),default=0)
    return rows,raw,res
for asat,b in ((ASAT_CW,"CW"),(ASAT_PULSE,"pulse")):
  for cl,bnd in CORNERS:
    rows,raw,res=ledger(asat,BULK,bnd)
    print("%-5s %-11s bulk%.2f+bnd%.2f: raw=%d res=%d | "%(b,cl,BULK,bnd,raw,res)
          +" ".join("F%d:%+.2f"%(F,m) for F,m in rows))
# (C) disorder regeneration-floor fan-out ceiling + MC literature-puddle yield
# (feed the fan-out-softened k_eff into disorder.py's design-sharpness slot K0; in-model)
print("disorder floor k_min=%.1f: F<=(8/%.0f)^2=%.2f => F_max=%d"
      %(D.K_MIN, D.K_MIN, (8.0/D.K_MIN)**2, int((8.0/D.K_MIN)**2)))
for F in (1,2,3,4):
    kf=keff(F); D.K0=kf
    y=D.yield_fraction(1e12, 2.5e9, 4e10) if kf>D.K_MIN else 0.0   # lit puddle 2.5e9..4e10 cm^-2
    print("  F=%d k_eff=%.3f clears? %-3s lit-puddle yield=%.0f%%"
          %(F,kf,"yes" if kf>=D.K_MIN else "NO",100*y))
D.K0=8.0
print("ISI: %.4f dB@0.25THz %.4f dB@1THz"%(CELL.isi_spread_dB(0.25e12),CELL.isi_spread_dB(1.0e12)))
```

4. **Bulk term carried at its central −0.95 dB with band [−1.5, −0.3].** The band alone moves the near-specular margin-reserved F_max between 1 and 2 (§6/E in the working driver) — F = 2 is not robust to the bulk term's *own* promoted uncertainty, let alone the contact gate.

6. **Depth D is not quantified to a number.** The static chain carries no per-stage BER/jitter model; D is stated structurally (noise/disorder-limited, gain-unbounded once margin>0) with the number left open.
7. **Everything rides on the unproven gain cell** (bench gate G1).
