# Charter of the Fable Computer Project

*Draft — takes effect when adopted by the maintainer as the project's first
recorded decision. Amendments follow the process defined in
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).*

## 1. Mission

The Fable Computer project exists to answer, honestly and in the open, one
question: **can a regenerative graphene-plasmon fabric compute at terahertz
rates at room temperature?** The project develops the architecture, the
models, the simulations, and ultimately the pre-registered bench experiments
that answer it — pass or fail — and publishes everything it learns so that
anyone can verify, extend, or refute the work.

## 2. Purpose and non-distribution

The project is a **non-profit research collective**. It exists to produce and
freely publish scientific knowledge, not to generate profit.

- No part of the project's funds or assets is ever distributed to members as
  profit, dividend, or token. Funds are spent only on the project's research
  purpose (compute, bounties, equipment, experiments, infrastructure,
  contracted work), through the process defined in
  [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md).
- Membership confers a voice in decisions. It is earned by contribution,
  cannot be bought, and carries no financial interest of any kind.

## 3. Scope of version 1: simple tools, no crypto

Version 1 of this community deliberately uses **no blockchain, no
cryptocurrency, no tokens, and no smart contracts**. Governance and treasury
records are plain, human-readable files in this repository; the git history is
the audit trail; funds move on ordinary fiat rails through a fiscal host with
a public ledger.

Revisiting this choice would be a constitutional amendment: it requires a
75% supermajority of voting members through the amendment process, and is out
of scope until a substantial community exists to make that choice.

## 4. Principles

1. **Radical transparency.** Models, data, decisions, and money are public.
   Anything that cannot be published (private personal data, a partner lab's
   confidential terms) is minimized and its existence acknowledged.
2. **Honest uncertainty.** Every claim carries its epistemic status:
   *demonstrated* (measured, published), *in-model* (established within a
   stated model chain under stated assumptions), or *open*. Negative results
   and failed replications are first-class, credited contributions. The
   project's claims are offered to be falsified; pre-registered pass/fail
   criteria are fixed before experiments run.
3. **Defensive publication.** Everything is published openly, permanently, and
   citably, so that no one — including this project — can enclose it behind
   patents. See [LICENSING.md](LICENSING.md).
4. **Membership is earned, not bought.** There is nothing to purchase and no
   fee to join. Contributors who do the work gain a recorded voice.
5. **Simple, boring, redundant infrastructure.** Governance survives any
   single platform's death: records live in the repository and are mirrored to
   independent archives (DOI snapshots), never only on a third-party service.

## 5. Safety and physical experiments

The collective funds and publishes **computation, design, analysis, and
pre-registered protocols**. Physical experiments carried out toward the
project's bench gates run inside host laboratories under those institutions'
own safety, ethics, and liability regimes, under written agreement — never as
informal community lab work. The project will adopt a detailed safety and
replication policy before any experiment runs under its name.

## 6. Relationship to the founder

The project was founded by Ryoji Furui, who serves as maintainer under
[GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md). This charter binds the maintainer like any
member: the maintainer's authority is defined there, is exercised in public,
and narrows automatically as the community grows past the written thresholds.
